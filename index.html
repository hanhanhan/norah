Meet Norah

Norah is a professional joke-teller, modern-dancer, ice cream-licker, tree-climber, and loving big sister. At four years old, she takes her jobs pretty seriously. And she's not messing around when it comes to chocolate chip cookies.

When Norah's not in Pre-K, you can find her playing in her favorite park or lounging at home with her nose in a book (or ten).

What's FA?

Norah was diagnosed with a rare genetic disorder called Fanconi Anemia (FA). Essentially, FA interferes with her body's ability to properly repair its DNA, which increases her risk of leukemia or bone marrow failure to 90%, if untreated.

Currently, the only treatment to prevent leukemia is a bone marrow transplant. Norah is currently healthy, but her doctors predict that she will need a bone marrow transplant in the next few years which will require a donor with an exact match to make sure the procedure is successful.

Finding the right bone marrow match is not easy. While siblings typically make the best donors, Norah's little brother is unfortunately not a match. Norah's doctors have begun searching the national bone marrow registry through the organization Be The Match to search for a donor match who can save her life.

This is where you come in.

We know that the likelihood of finding a bone marrow match increases substantially along ancestry lines. For Norah,this means that we are looking primarily for a donor with Jewish Ashkenazi ancestry.

But we also know that many other groups are severely under-represented in the bone marrow registry, resulting in low odds of finding a donor. So we are in this to help Norah and all children who need a life-saving match.

As you can imagine, it is a leap of faith to have your child's life depend on the generosity of others.

We love Norah with all of our hearts, and we are eternally grateful for your generosity and support.
